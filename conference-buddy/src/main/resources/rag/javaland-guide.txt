JavaLand 2026 Conference Guide — Europa-Park, Rust, Germany

§A Venue & Logistics
A.1 JavaLand 2026 takes place at Europa-Park, Europa-Park-Str. 2, 77977 Rust, Germany.
A.2 Conference dates are March 9-12, 2026. Monday March 9 is the Unconference at Hotel Santa Isabel from 3:00 pm to 6:30 pm.
A.3 Tuesday March 10 and Wednesday March 11 are the main conference days with talks, keynotes, and community sessions. The conference takes place in the Europa-Park Confertainment Center.
A.4 Thursday March 12 is the training day with full-day hands-on workshops. These require separate registration (All-in-One ticket).
A.5 Hotel parking is free for guests. Day visitor parking at the Confertainment Center costs €29 per day.

§B Session Rooms
B.1 Dome — The main stage used for keynotes and high-capacity talks. All Dome sessions are streamed live via the JavaLand website and heise.
B.2 Bellevue — Architecture & Methodology sessions; a mid-sized room for design and process talks.
B.3 Sanssouci — Java Language & Community track; great for core Java and ecosystem discussions.
B.4 Salle Petit Paris — Mixed topics room covering a broad range of subjects.
B.5 Versailles — Software Engineering track; focused on practices, testing, and craft.
B.6 CircusCelebration — AI & Testing talks; the go-to room for artificial intelligence and quality topics.
B.7 Das Studio — DevOps & Tooling sessions; build systems, CI/CD, and developer productivity.
B.8 Traumpalast — Hands-on workshops and extended coding sessions.
B.9 Jeanne d'Arc — Extended sessions and deep-dive talks that run longer than the standard slot.
B.10 Magic Cinema — Security & Advanced topics; cryptography, application security, and advanced JVM features.
B.11 Hackergarten (Foyer Galerie) — Community coding space where attendees contribute to open-source projects together. Also home to the Dev Lounge for networking between talks.
B.12 Goethe — Home of the legendary Mario Kart tournament.
B.13 Heinrich Mack — Speaker Lounge and home of board games and Magic: The Gathering evenings.

§C Evening Activities & Community Events
C.1 OpenPark evening event — Tuesday March 10. Dinner is included. See the OpenPark page on javaland.eu for details.
C.2 Mario Kart Tournament — Tuesday 11:00-21:30 in the Goethe room. Sign up with Stefan Nachtrab! A JavaLand tradition with fierce competition.
C.3 Magic: The Gathering — Tuesday and Wednesday evenings in Heinrich Mack. Bring your decks or borrow from the community collection.
C.4 Board Games Night — Tuesday and Wednesday evenings in Heinrich Mack. A wide selection of board games for a relaxed evening.
C.5 Java macht Musik! — Tuesday 17:30-19:00 in Hackergarten (Foyer Galerie). Live music performed by members of the Java community.
C.6 Unconference — Monday March 9 from 3:00 pm to 6:30 pm at the Hotel Santa Isabel. A relaxed exchange among like-minded people before the main conference.

§D Europa-Park Rides (for between sessions!)
D.1 Europa-Park is one of Europe's largest theme parks. Conference attendees can enjoy park attractions during breaks.
D.2 Silver Star — One of Europe's tallest and fastest steel coasters, reaching 130 km/h.
D.3 Blue Fire Megacoaster — A launched steel coaster with multiple inversions in the Iceland area.
D.4 Wodan Timburcoaster — One of Europe's longest wooden roller coasters in the Germany area.
D.5 Arthur — An indoor family coaster adventure through the world of Arthur and the Minimoys.
D.6 Poseidon — A thrilling water coaster. You will get wet!
D.7 Eurosat CanCan Coaster — A dark indoor coaster with neon lights and cancan music.

§E Food & Drinks
E.1 Unlimited coffee, water, and apple spritzer are available all day at the conference venue.
E.2 Morning coffee break from 9:00 am to 10:30 am with snacks and fruit.
E.3 Lunch is included with your conference ticket and served from 11:30 am to 2:15 pm. Options: Flammkuchen stand, Walters Wurstbude (Urwurst, Flieger Fries, Poutine), Burger Bär (Cheeseburger, Hamburger, French fries), Marios Pizza (Margherita and Salami slices), Chez Marianne (ice cream).
E.4 Afternoon coffee break from 3:30 pm to 4:30 pm with snacks and fruit.
E.5 Dinner is only available on Tuesday March 10 as part of the OpenPark evening event.

§F Registration & Tickets
F.1 Tickets can be viewed, downloaded, and scanned digitally via your DOAG profile at meine.doag.org under "Tickets & Rooms".
F.2 Physical tickets can be printed at home; badges with lanyards are provided on-site.
F.3 The registration desk is at the main entrance of the Confertainment Center.
F.4 Registration desk hours: Tuesday March 10 from 7:30 am to 11:00 am; Wednesday March 11 from 8:00 am to 11:00 am.
F.5 On-site ticket purchase is possible but All-in-One training day slots may be full — no guarantee of availability.
F.6 Training day workshop selection can be changed up to seven days before the event if spots remain.

§G Hotels & Getting There
G.1 Europa-Park partner hotels: Krønasår, Bell Rock, El Andaluz, and Colosseo.
G.2 Hotel parking is free for guests. Day visitor parking at the Confertainment Center costs €29 per day.
G.3 Electric car charging: four points each at Krønasår, Bell Rock, and El Andaluz; twelve at Colosseo. Two fast chargers at Colosseo and Bell Rock, and between Krønasår hotel and Rulantica.
G.4 Online hotel check-in is available via the Europa-Park Hotels app on the day of arrival.
G.5 A shuttle service runs between the partner hotels and the conference venue. See the "Getting there" page on javaland.eu for the full schedule.

§H Practical Tips
H.1 WiFi is available in all session rooms. All rooms have a projector and screen or large monitor. Laptops are not provided — bring your own. MacBook users should bring an adapter.
H.2 The Dev Lounge at the Hackergarten (Foyer Galerie) is a great spot for networking and coding between talks.
H.3 Don't miss the Community Keynote on Wednesday at 11:00 in the Dome — it features live awards and audience voting.
H.4 The Speaker Lounge is at Heinrich Mack — a quiet space to prepare before your talk. Speakers receive an exclusive JavaLand hoodie at the Infocorner.
H.5 All sessions (except workshops) are recorded and available free of charge to all participants shortly after the event via the agenda.
H.6 The JavaLand Studio is in the Dome and live-streams all Dome sessions via the website and heise.
H.7 Wear comfortable shoes — you will be walking between areas to reach different session rooms.

§I Infocorner & Services
I.1 The Infocorner is near the main entrance of the Confertainment Center, next to the stairs.
I.2 Infocorner hours: Tuesday March 10 from 7:30 am to 6:00 pm; Wednesday March 11 from 8:00 am to 5:00 pm.
I.3 A merchandise booth with JavaLand goodies is in the exhibition area.
I.4 A wardrobe is available in the exhibition area — check the park map for the exact location and times.
I.5 After hotel check-out, leave luggage at the hotel until departure if possible.
