JavaLand 2026 Conference Guide — Europa-Park, Rust, Germany

§A Venue & Logistics
A.1 JavaLand 2026 takes place at Europa-Park, Europa-Park-Str. 2, 77977 Rust, Germany.
A.2 Conference dates are March 9-12, 2026. Monday March 9 is the Unconference at Hotel Santa Isabel from 3:00 pm to 6:30 pm.
A.3 Tuesday March 10 and Wednesday March 11 are the main conference days with talks, keynotes, and community sessions. The conference takes place in the Europa-Park Confertainment Center.
A.4 Thursday March 12 is the training day with full-day hands-on workshops. These require separate registration (All-in-One ticket).
A.5 The venue is Europa-Park in Rust, Germany. Hotel guests can use free hotel parking; day visitor parking at the Confertainment Center costs €29 per day.

§B Session Rooms
B.1 Dome — The main stage used for keynotes and high-capacity talks.
B.2 Bellevue — Architecture & Methodology sessions; a mid-sized room for design and process talks.
B.3 Sanssouci — Java Language & Community track; great for core Java and ecosystem discussions.
B.4 Salle Petit Paris — Mixed topics room covering a broad range of subjects.
B.5 Versailles — Software Engineering track; focused on practices, testing, and craft.
B.6 CircusCelebration — AI & Testing talks; the go-to room for artificial intelligence and quality topics.
B.7 Das Studio — DevOps & Tooling sessions; build systems, CI/CD, and developer productivity.
B.8 Traumpalast — Hands-on workshops and extended coding sessions.
B.9 Jeanne d'Arc — Extended sessions and deep-dive talks that run longer than the standard slot.
B.10 Magic Cinema — Security & Advanced topics; cryptography, application security, and advanced JVM features.
B.11 Hackergarten (Foyer Galerie) — Community coding space where attendees contribute to open-source projects together.
B.12 Goethe — Home of the legendary Mario Kart tournament.
B.13 Heinrich Mack — Board games and Magic: The Gathering evenings.

§H Practical Tips
H.1 WiFi is available in all session rooms. All lecture rooms are equipped with Wi-Fi, a projector and screen, or a large monitor.
H.2 The Dev Lounge at the Hackergarten (Foyer Galerie) is a great spot for networking and coding between talks.
H.3 Don't miss the Community Keynote on Wednesday at 11:00 in the Dome — it features live awards and audience voting.
H.4 Thursday workshops require separate registration (All-in-One ticket). Selection can be changed up to seven days before the event.
H.5 Wear comfortable shoes — you will be walking between areas to reach different session rooms.
H.6 The JavaLand app and DOAG profile have the full schedule with room maps and personal agenda/favorites features.
H.7 Laptops are not provided in session rooms. If you use a MacBook, bring an adapter.
H.8 The Speaker Lounge is at Heinrich Mack — a quiet space to prepare before your talk.
H.9 All sessions (except workshops) will be recorded and made available free of charge to all participants afterwards.
H.10 The JavaLand Studio is located in the Dome and streams all Dome sessions live via the website and heise.
